STUR QC – Resistance Flow Engine

Lattice entanglement, resistance flux, and continuity in the STUR framework

Derivation Sketch

In the STUR picture, “resistance” is promoted from a scalar to a full quantum field living on a lattice of sites. For a one-dimensional chain of N two-level sites we define Pauli operators σx(i), σy(i), σz(i) acting on site i, and the usual ladder operators σ+, σ−.

S(r;L0) = tanh(r / L0) · (1 − e−r/L0)

is the STUR radial modulator: it suppresses short-range pathologies near r = 0 and saturates to 1 for r ≫ L0. On a 1D lattice with spacing alat, positions are ri = i · alat. The effective STUR “resistance generator” is

RSTUR = ∑i h0 S(ri) σz(i) + ∑i<j J0 S(|ri−rj|) (σx(i)σx(j) + σy(i)σy(j)) + Ω ∑i S(ri) σx(i).

Instead of the Born-rule interpretation of the wavefunction, we treat Ψ(t) as a deterministic resistance field obeying

iħ dΨ/dt = RSTUR Ψ.

From Ψ(t) we compute:

  • Local “resistance density” ρi(t) = ⟨ni⟩ = ⟨(1 − σz(i))/2⟩.
  • Bond currents Ji→j(t) = 4 Jij Im ⟨σ+i σ−j⟩ with Jij = J0 S(|ri−rj|).
  • A lattice continuity equation, dρi/dt + Σj Ji→j ≈ 0, which we check numerically.
  • Entanglement entropy of a block A, SA(t) = −Tr[ρA log₂ ρA], and a global “resistance flux”

ΦR(t) = Re [ Ψ† RSTUR Ψ ].

This notebook does two runs:

  1. A full RSTUR evolution for N = 6 sites, reporting the growth of entanglement and drift of ΦR.
  2. A continuity test with XX+YY couplings only, starting from a single excitation on site 0. This produces:
    • A ρi(t) heatmap (density flow)
    • A bond current trace J0→1(t)
    • Global invariants SA(t) and ΦR(t)
    • A compact JSON summary for GitHub / Zenodo
