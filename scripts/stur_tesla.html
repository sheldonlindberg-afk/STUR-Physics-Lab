STUR Tesla Dream – Solar Beam Space Power System

Physical Derivation and Model

We model a Solar Beam Space Power (SBSP) system composed of six orbital fusion-hybrid photovoltaic satellites transmitting wireless power to Earth. Each satellite captures solar flux Φ⊙ ≈ 1360 W/m² over an effective area A ≈ 3.17 km². Combined conversion efficiency:

η_total = η_PV × η_DC→RF × η_TX × η_RX ≈ 0.60 × 0.95 × 0.80 × 0.90 × 0.85 ≈ 0.35.

The generated grid power is P_grid = Φ⊙ A η_total n_sat ≈ 13.7 GW. Temporal factors modify this base:

  • Eclipse modulation (Earth shadow, 20 min/day)
  • Doppler shift (+10⁻⁵ sinusoidal)
  • Shapiro delay (−10⁻⁴ GR correction)
  • Ionospheric attenuation (0.94 – 1.0)
  • Fresnel interference loss (~5%)
  • Superconducting relay (~0.999)
  • Weather factor via barometric and thermal AI model
The product yields the instantaneous transmitted power P(t) in GW.

The Ca Bio-Battery stores surplus energy with energy density E_b = 3364 Wh/kg and decay constant k_d = ln 2 / (69 days). The simulation integrates hourly supply, decay, and demand to yield the state-of-charge (SoC).

Python Simulation (JupyterLite-safe)

#!/usr/bin/env python3
# ===============================================================
# STUR Tesla Dream – SBSP + Ca Bio-Battery Model
# ===============================================================
import numpy as np, matplotlib.pyplot as plt
from scipy.special import fresnel

# --- Parameters ---
solar_flux = 1360
area = 3.17e6
pv_eff = 0.60 * 0.95
dc_to_rf, tx_eff, rx_eff = 0.80, 0.90, 0.85
overall_eff = pv_eff * dc_to_rf * tx_eff * rx_eff
n_sat = 6
P_grid = solar_flux * area * overall_eff * n_sat  # ≈13.7 GW

# --- Time vector (7 days, 15 min) ---
t_hours = np.linspace(0, 7*24, 7*24*4)

# --- Eclipse modulation ---
eclipse = np.ones_like(t_hours)
for day in range(7):
    if day in [2, 3, 4]:
        t_ecl = (t_hours - day*24) % 24
        mask = (t_ecl >= 1) & (t_ecl < 1.333)
        eclipse[mask] = np.cos(np.pi*(t_ecl[mask]-1.167)/0.333)**2
eclipse = np.clip(eclipse, 0, 1)

# --- Transmission modifiers ---
doppler = 1 + 1e-5 * np.sin(2*np.pi*t_hours/24)
shapiro = 1 - 1e-4 * (1 + 0.1*np.sin(2*np.pi*t_hours/24))
qec = 1.03

# --- Atmospheric/weather model ---
P = 101325 - 100*np.sin(2*np.pi*t_hours/12)
T = 290 + 10*np.sin(2*np.pi*t_hours/12)
weather = np.clip(0.985 + 0.015*(P/101325)*(T/290), 0.97, 1.0)
iono = np.clip(0.97 + 0.03*np.sin(2*np.pi*t_hours/12)*np.random.randn(len(t_hours)), 0.94, 1.0)
fresnel_loss = 0.95*(1 - 0.05*np.abs(fresnel(t_hours/168)[0]))
supercon_relay = 0.999

# --- Total transmitted power ---
power_output = (P_grid * eclipse * doppler * shapiro * qec *
                iono * fresnel_loss * supercon_relay * weather)

# --- Ca Bio-Battery parameters ---
E_bat = 3364  # Wh/kg
kd = np.log(2)/(69*24)
daily_demand = 3.52 * 24
backup_demand = daily_demand * 90
m_bat = backup_demand * 1e6 / E_bat * np.exp(kd*90*24)
SoC = np.zeros_like(t_hours)
SoC[0] = daily_demand
for i in range(1, len(t_hours)):
    P_in = power_output[i]*0.25
    SoC_prev = SoC[i-1]*np.exp(-kd*0.25)
    SoC[i] = min(SoC_prev + P_in - daily_demand/(24*4), daily_demand)

# --- Plots ---
fig, ax = plt.subplots(1, 2, figsize=(10,4))
ax[0].plot(t_hours/24, power_output)
ax[0].set(xlabel="Days", ylabel="Power (GW)",
          title="6-Sat SBSP with Fusion Hybrids")
ax[0].grid(True)
ax[1].plot(t_hours/24, SoC)
ax[1].set(xlabel="Days", ylabel="SoC (GWh)", title="Ca Bio-Battery SoC")
ax[1].grid(True)
plt.tight_layout(); plt.show()

# --- Summary ---
mean_out = P_grid * iono.mean() * fresnel_loss.mean() * supercon_relay * weather.mean()
energy_TWh = mean_out*24*365/1000
print(f"6 Satellites: Each ≈ 3.17 km², Total {mean_out:.1f} GW, {energy_TWh:.0f} TWh/year")
print(f"Ca Battery: {m_bat/1e6:.1f} M tonnes for {backup_demand:.0f} GWh (90 days)")
